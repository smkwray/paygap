Methodology

Data sources, sample definitions, estimation approaches, and robustness checks.

1. Data Sources

Dataset Agency Years Role N per Year
ACS PUMS Census Bureau 2015-2019, 2021-2023 Primary wage estimates ~900K
CPS ASEC Census/BLS 2015-2023 Cross-check + selection ~50-60K
SIPP Census Bureau 2023 Additional cross-check 182,658
ATUS BLS Pooled Time-use mechanisms Various
SCE NY Fed Public chart series Wage expectations Various
NLSY79/97 BLS Cohort Background + skills ~2,500

2. Sample Definition

  • Age 25-54 (prime working age)
  • Wage/salary workers only
  • Positive hours and positive pay
  • Excludes active-duty military and self-employed

3. Estimation Approaches

Method Description
Sequential OLS (M0-M5) Progressively adds control blocks; primary approach
Oaxaca-Blinder Decomposes gap into explained and unexplained components
DML Double/debiased ML with elastic net; sensitivity check
IPW Inverse probability weighting for employment selection
SDR Successive-difference replication for survey uncertainty

4. ACS Control Blocks

Block Controls Added
M0 Female indicator only
M1 + age, age², race/ethnicity, education
M2 + state
M3 + occupation, industry, class of worker
M4 + usual hours, work from home, commute
M5 + marital status, children, children under 5

5. Survey Uncertainty

  • ACS: 80 replicate weights, successive-difference replication
  • Average 90% margin of error: 0.29 percentage points
  • Raw gap SE: ~0.19 pp (2023)
  • M5 female coefficient SE: 0.0017

5b. Reproductive Extension (M6–M8)

Block Controls Added
M6 + recent birth, recent marriage, own child under 6, own child 6–17, couple type, reproductive stage
M7 + O*NET context: autonomy, schedule unpredictability, time pressure, coordination responsibility, physical proximity, job rigidity
M8 + female × recent birth, female × child under 6, female × recent marriage, female × same-sex, female × autonomy, female × job rigidity, female × child under 6 × job rigidity

5c. Variance Suite (V1–V4)

Suite Description
V1 Raw and residual variance ratios, P90/P10, P95/P50, top-earner shares by gender
V2 Selection-corrected variance (IPW reweighting for employment probability)
V3 Variance by reproductive stage (childless, recently married, mothers by child age)
V4 Variance by O*NET job-rigidity quartile

6. Robustness Checks

  • Employment selection (IPW reweighting)
  • DML with elastic net
  • Oaxaca decomposition
  • SIPP validation (including reproductive stage gaps)
  • CPS cross-check
  • ACS family field correction
  • Year-by-year stability (8 ACS years)
  • Quantile regression
  • Same-sex placebo / lesbian married premium (negative control)
  • Fertility-risk gradient among childless women
  • NLSY79/97 cohort validation of reproductive controls

7. Limitations

  • No firm/employer effects
  • No tenure or work-history interruption data
  • No direct bargaining/negotiation variables
  • No within-occupation task specialization
  • No schedule flexibility measures
  • ACS 2020 unavailable due to COVID collection issues
