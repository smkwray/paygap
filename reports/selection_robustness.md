# CPS Selection Robustness

This note summarizes the CPS ASEC employment-selection robustness layer.

## Headline pattern (S2 block)
- Years covered: 2015-2023
- Mean female employment probability effect: 1.23 percentage points
- Mean combined expected annual earnings gap: 31.99%
- Mean observed total annual earnings gap: 27.43%
- Mean observed worker hourly wage gap: 19.51%
- Mean IPW worker hourly wage gap: 19.25%

## Interpretation
- The CPS worker wage gap and the total annual earnings gap are different estimands.
- The two-part expected-earnings gap is consistently larger than the worker-only hourly wage gap.
- The IPW worker wage gap is close to, but not identical to, the observed worker gap; that is evidence that labor-force selection matters, but it does not dominate the worker-gap result.
